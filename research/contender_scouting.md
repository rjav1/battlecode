## Executive Summary

The contender tier (ranks 4-15, Elo ~2180-2390) is a densely packed field where teams trade wins against each other in close 3-2 matches, but are almost universally dominated by the top 3 (Blue Dragon, Kessoku Band, something else). The critical gap between Master and Grandmaster tiers is **dual-competency**: top teams win through BOTH military aggression (Core Destroyed in <400 turns) AND economic superiority (Resource Victory at 2000 turns). Contender-tier teams can do one or the other, but rarely both consistently.

## Win Condition Analysis

### How Games End

Based on detailed match data from multiple match pages:

**Core Destroyed (Military Kill):**
- Fast kills (100-400 turns): Dominant teams ending it quickly on favorable maps
- Medium kills (400-1000 turns): Contested pushes where one side eventually overwhelms
- Long kills (1000-2000 turns): Extended warfare, often after failed economy plays

**Resource Victory (2000 turn tiebreaker):**
- Always exactly 2000 turns
- This is the ECONOMY win condition -- whoever has more resources at turn 2000 wins
- Top teams win Resource Victory consistently because their economy is superior

### The Critical Insight: Dual Competency

**Blue Dragon 5-0 over MFF1:** default_large2 (RV 2000t), hourglass (CD 407t), gaussian (CD 834t), landscape (RV 2000t), wasteland_oasis (RV 2000t)
- Blue Dragon won 3 games via economy AND 2 via military

**Kessoku Band 5-0 over MFF1:** arena (CD 222t), hourglass (CD 148t), sierpinski_evil (RV 2000t), settlement (RV 2000t), face (RV 2000t)
- Kessoku Band won 2 games via fast military AND 3 via economy

**something else 4-1 over MFF1:** mandelbrot (CD 630t), socket (RV 2000t), thread_of_connection (CD 223t), minimaze (RV 2000t), galaxy (CD 1224t -- MFF1 won)
- something else won 2 games via military AND 2 via economy

**Contender vs Contender (bwaaa 3-2 MFF1):** starry_night (CD 366t), corridors (CD 324t), binary_tree (CD 331t -- MFF1 won), arena (RV 2000t -- MFF1 won), gaussian (CD 215t)
- ALL games decided by Core Destroyed or one Resource Victory. The match was almost entirely military-focused.

### The Gap Explained

**Top teams (2400+ Elo)** can:
1. Rush and kill cores in under 400 turns on aggressive maps
2. Out-economy opponents to win Resource Victory on defensive/large maps
3. Adapt per-map to choose the right approach

**Contender teams (2200-2400 Elo)** typically can:
1. Rush effectively on SOME maps (often winning Core Destroyed in 200-400t)
2. BUT they lose the economy game on maps that go to 2000t
3. OR they play economy-focused but can't close out military wins quickly enough

This means on a Bo5 with mixed map types, top teams can win the military maps AND the economy maps, while contenders can only reliably win one type.

## Common Patterns Across Contenders

### What They Do Well
1. **Military rushes on aggressive maps** -- Most contenders can kill cores in 200-400 turns on maps like hourglass, corridors, gaussian, starry_night
2. **Beat weaker teams consistently** -- All contenders handle teams below ~2150 Elo easily
3. **Close matches against peers** -- The 3-2 scoreline is the most common result between same-tier teams

### What They Do Poorly
1. **Economy game vs top teams** -- When maps go to 2000t Resource Victory, contenders almost always lose to top 3
2. **Consistency** -- Huge Elo swings (100+ points in a day) suggest strategy fragility
3. **Adaptation** -- Several teams peaked weeks ago and are declining, suggesting they haven't updated their bots to counter the evolving meta

## Key Takeaways

1. **The gap is ECONOMY.** Contender-tier teams can rush on favorable maps, but they all lose the Resource Victory tiebreaker against top teams. Improving turn-2000 resource generation is the single highest-leverage improvement.

2. **MFF1 is the gatekeeper.** Any team aspiring to Grandmaster must be able to beat MFF1 consistently. They're the best of the contender tier.

3. **bwaaa has the highest ceiling** (peak 2432) but also the most variance. They've shown they can beat anyone... and lose to anyone.

4. **Dear jump proves solo can work** -- don't underestimate focused single-developer teams.

5. **Several contenders are declining** (Oxford, Dear jump, bwaaa). The meta is shifting and teams that don't adapt fall. Active development is critical.

6. **The Bo5 format with mixed maps is key** -- you need to win at least 3 of 5, and map types will vary. A one-dimensional strategy caps out around 2300 Elo.

7. **Timeline is tight** -- International qualifier is April 20, only ~2 weeks away. Every sprint counts.
