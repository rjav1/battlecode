## Executive Summary

We have 14 days to climb from ~1488 to competitive qualifier range. Our bot is a solid economy-first player with d.opposite() conveyors, sector exploration, and gunner defense. But our nemesis analysis reveals two structural weaknesses: (1) disconnected harvester chains hemorrhaging Ti, and (2) resource hoarding (3-18K Ti sitting idle). Below are 7 novel strategies ordered by expected impact, each grounded in game mechanics and designed to avoid known dead ends.

## Strategy 1: Conveyor Chain Auditing via Markers ("Chain Census")

### What is it?
Each harvester, on its first output tick (immediate, then every 4 rounds), places a marker on its own tile with a unique ID-encoded value. Builders periodically scan visible conveyors: if a conveyor has no resource stack flowing through it after N rounds, the builder traces backward from that conveyor to find the break point and patches it. Essentially, use markers as a distributed "network health monitor."

### Why might it work?
Our nemesis data shows we build 30-43 harvesters but collect 2-3x LESS Ti than opponents with 8-22 harvesters. The problem isn't harvester count -- it's that most harvesters are disconnected. Currently we have zero visibility into which chains are working. A chain audit system would let builders prioritize fixing broken chains over building new harvesters, converting dead infrastructure into live revenue.

The d.opposite() approach builds conveyors facing back toward core, but diagonal movement creates gaps. Rather than fixing the diagonal gap problem (proven dead end), we detect and patch only the chains that are actually broken.

### Risk
- CPU cost of scanning conveyors each round (mitigate: only audit every 10 rounds, only builders with id%3==0)
- Marker slots are limited (1 per round per unit) -- but harvesters don't use markers for anything else currently
- Complexity of "trace backward" logic

### How to test
1. Build a "chain_audit" bot variant that counts visible harvesters vs. visible conveyors-with-resources
2. Run on cold, galaxy, landscape (our worst economy maps)
3. Compare Ti collected at round 2000 vs baseline v35

### Expected Elo impact
+30 to +60 Elo. This directly attacks our #1 weakness (disconnected chains account for 2-3x Ti gap). Even a 50% improvement in chain connectivity would be transformative.

### Implementation sketch
```
# In _builder, every 10 rounds:
# 1. Scan nearby conveyors within vision
# 2. For each allied conveyor, check c.get_stored_resource(id)
# 3. If conveyor is empty AND has been empty for 2+ checks, it's "dead"
# 4. Trace the conveyor's facing direction backward to find source
# 5. If source is harvester but chain is broken, set self.target = break point
# 6. Navigate there and build a conveyor patch
```

## Strategy 2: Aggressive Reinvestment Curve ("Spend Down")

### What is it?
Instead of accumulating Ti and spending based on static thresholds, implement a dynamic spending policy: when Ti exceeds a threshold (say 200 + 50*harvester_count), immediately convert excess into more builders, gunners, or barriers. The key insight: **banked Ti counts toward the tiebreaker BUT only if we're already losing the economy race**. If we're ahead, spending it on infrastructure compounds our advantage.

### Why might it work?
We hoard 3-18K Ti while One More Time keeps <300 in bank. Every 30 Ti sitting in the bank could have been a builder finding new ore. Every 10 Ti could have been a gunner defending harvesters. The spending hypothesis was marked as a dead end because "bank balance IS part of Ti collected" -- but this misunderstands the mechanic. **Delivered Ti** (resources that reach the core) counts for tiebreaker. Ti sitting in bank is NOT the same as Ti that was generated by harvesters and delivered. Spending Ti on builders that find more ore creates a multiplicative effect: each new connected harvester generates ~2.5 Ti/round (10 Ti every 4 rounds = stack of 10, but only if connected).

The real question is: does spending 30 Ti on a builder generate more than 30 Ti over the remaining game? If the builder finds ore within 50 rounds and builds a connected harvester, that harvester produces ~2.5 Ti/round = 125 Ti over 50 more rounds. The ROI is massive.

### Risk
- Overspending in early game when Ti income is low (mitigate: only activate after round 100 and 2+ harvesters)
- More builders = more scale cost = more expensive everything (mitigate: cap at 15-20 builders, not 40+)
- On maps with little ore, extra builders find nothing (mitigate: map_mode awareness)

### How to test
1. Create "spend_down" variant with aggressive builder spawning when Ti > threshold
2. Run on landscape, default_large2, wasteland (economy-focused maps)
3. Track Ti-collected-at-core separately from bank balance

### Expected Elo impact
+15 to +30 Elo. Medium impact because it amplifies our existing economy rather than fixing the core chain problem. Best combined with Strategy 1.

## Strategy 3: Splitter-Fed Gunner Arrays ("Ammo Network")

### What is it?
Build a dedicated ammo supply chain: Core -> conveyor line -> splitter -> 3 gunners. One splitter can feed 3 gunners alternating between its 3 forward outputs. The splitter accepts from behind only, so position it with "behind" facing toward core (where conveyors deliver Ti). Each gunner needs 2 Ti per shot, and a splitter distributes stacks of 10 Ti across 3 gunners.

### Why might it work?
Our gunners currently have no ammo supply -- they fire once (if pre-loaded?) and sit idle. An unarmed gunner is 10 Ti + 10% scale wasted. The splitter integration was "proven but never properly integrated" (per HANDOFF.md priority #5). Armed gunners transform from decoration to area denial. A single armed gunner does 10 damage per round -- it can kill an enemy builder bot (30 HP) in 3 rounds, preventing enemy expansion toward our side. Against pure economy bots like One More Time (ZERO turrets), even 2-3 armed gunners would be devastating.

Key mechanic detail: Gunner fires along forward ray. It auto-targets closest entity on that line. If we place gunners facing toward the enemy core, they deny the entire corridor.

### Risk
- Splitter costs 6 Ti + 1% scale -- modest
- Need to route conveyors to feed the splitter, which means diverting from core delivery
- Gunner rotation costs 10 Ti -- so initial facing must be correct (toward enemy)
- Friendly conveyors/buildings in the fire line block shots

### How to test
1. Build "armed_gunner" variant with splitter ammo pattern
2. Test vs ladder_rush (should improve defense) and ladder_eco (should deny their expansion)
3. Key metric: enemy builder bot kills + territory denied

### Expected Elo impact
+20 to +40 Elo. This is a proven concept awaiting integration. Against zero-defense economy bots (significant portion of the meta), armed gunners are game-changing.

## Strategy 4: Sentinel Stun-Lock Perimeter ("Stun Wall")

### What is it?
Place 2-3 sentinels near core, facing outward toward enemy approach vectors. Feed them refined axionite (requires foundry). With refined Ax ammo, sentinel hits add +5 to BOTH action AND move cooldown of directly-hit units. A stunned builder bot can't move or act for 5+ rounds. Place sentinels at distance 5-6 from core, covering the main approach corridors.

### Why might it work?
Sentinel has vision r^2=32 (huge) and hits all tiles within 1 king-move of the forward line. With stun, any enemy builder that enters our territory is frozen for 5 rounds. This is especially devastating against rush strategies like Polska Gurom's 319-turn core destroy. A stunned rushing builder can't attack our core, can't build, can't move. The 3-round reload means a sentinel fires every 3 rounds, and the stun lasts 5 rounds -- meaning a single sentinel can permanently lock down an approach corridor.

However: this requires foundry (100% scale increase!) and refined Ax ammo. The foundry tax is enormous.

### Risk
- **Foundry costs +100% scale** -- this is the single biggest risk. Building one foundry doubles the cost of everything thereafter. On maps where we're already behind on economy, this could be fatal.
- Requires Ti + Raw Ax inputs to foundry, meaning we need axionite ore mining
- Sentinel costs 30 Ti + 20% scale -- expensive unit
- If enemy doesn't rush, the stun is wasted on empty corridors

### How to test
1. Build "stun_sentinel" variant that builds 1 foundry + 1 sentinel with Ax ammo on tight maps only
2. Test specifically on face (where Polska Gurom rushed us at turn 319)
3. Compare: does the foundry+sentinel cost pay for itself by preventing core destruction?

### Expected Elo impact
+10 to +25 Elo on tight maps only. High risk due to foundry scale cost. Only viable as a conditional strategy on maps where rush is likely (tight maps with close cores). NOT recommended for economy maps.

## Strategy 5: Symmetry-Aware Ore Denial ("Mirror Mining")

### What is it?
Since maps are guaranteed symmetric (reflection or rotation), we can infer the location of every ore tile near the enemy's core by mirroring our own visible ore. Send 1-2 "denial" builders to the map center to build harvesters on ore tiles that are equidistant from both cores, denying them to the enemy. On symmetric maps, ore at the midpoint is contested -- whoever claims it first gets a permanent economy advantage.

### Why might it work?
Currently our builders explore outward from core in sectors, which means they naturally find ore near our side first. But ore at the map center is equally accessible to both teams, and it's first-come-first-served. If we send a fast builder straight to the center while others handle nearby ore, we can claim 2-3 contested ore tiles before the enemy reaches them. Each denied titanium ore represents ~2.5 Ti/round that the enemy loses AND we gain -- a 5 Ti/round swing per tile.

We already have symmetry detection code (_get_enemy_direction). We can compute mirror positions of our visible ore to identify where center ore should be.

### Risk
- The center-rushing builder produces nothing for 30-50 rounds while traveling
- If enemy has gunners near center, our builder dies (30 HP, no defense)
- Conveyors built at center may be too far from core to deliver efficiently
- Some maps have no center ore (all ore is near cores)

### How to test
1. Build "mirror_mine" variant: 3rd builder spawned targets map center instead of nearest ore
2. Run on landscape, default_large2, wasteland (maps with center ore)
3. Compare Ti collected AND enemy Ti collected (need to check tiebreaker data)

### Expected Elo impact
+10 to +20 Elo. Moderate impact, but it's a low-cost experiment (one builder reassigned). Biggest value on large, open maps with contested center ore.

## Strategy 6: Conveyor Topology Optimizer ("Hub and Spoke")

### What is it?
Instead of building independent chains from each harvester to core (which creates N parallel chains that may not connect), designate "trunk lines" -- 2-4 high-capacity conveyor highways from core outward in cardinal directions. All harvesters feed into the nearest trunk line rather than building their own chain back to core. Builders explore along trunk lines, branching off to reach ore tiles and connecting back with short "spoke" chains.

### Why might it work?
Our current d.opposite() approach builds conveyors as the builder walks, creating chains that follow the builder's winding path through obstacles. This produces long, tortuous chains with many direction changes and potential gaps. A hub-and-spoke model would create short, straight trunk lines first, then short branches to harvesters. Benefits:
- Fewer total conveyors needed (shared trunk reduces redundancy)
- Shorter branch chains = fewer gaps
- Easier to audit and repair (trunk lines are straight)
- Builder navigation follows trunk lines naturally (conveyors are walkable)

This is NOT roads-first (dead end). The trunk lines ARE conveyors, facing toward core. They serve dual purpose: resource transport AND builder walkways.

### Risk
- Requires coordination between builders (who builds trunk vs. who branches?)
- Trunk lines toward ore-poor directions are wasted
- More complex builder logic
- If trunk direction doesn't align with ore, branches get long anyway

### How to test
1. Build "hub_spoke" variant: first 2 builders walk in opposite cardinal directions from core, building conveyors
2. Subsequent builders walk along existing conveyors, branching when they see ore
3. Test on galaxy, cold, landscape (maps where our chain connectivity is worst)

### Expected Elo impact
+20 to +40 Elo if it works. This is architecturally ambitious -- high ceiling, high floor risk. The "if" is doing a lot of work. Recommend testing as a completely separate bot variant.

## Strategy 7: Timing Attack on Economy Transition ("Round 500 Power Spike")

### What is it?
Identify the round window (typically 400-600) when economy bots are most vulnerable: they've invested heavily in infrastructure but haven't yet reaped the full compound returns. At this moment, send 3-4 builder bots as attackers. Builder attack does 2 damage per hit (costs 2 Ti + action cooldown). Against a conveyor (20 HP), that's 10 hits to destroy. BUT: the builder can c.destroy() allied buildings for free, and the real value is disrupting the enemy's conveyor network. If we destroy 3-4 key conveyors in the enemy's trunk line, we disconnect multiple harvesters simultaneously.

Combine with launcher: throw an attacker builder over enemy defenses directly onto their conveyor network. Launcher has r^2=26 range and doesn't need ammo.

### Why might it work?
Wait -- launcher drops were listed as a dead end ("r^2=26 too short"). But let's re-examine: r^2=26 means the launcher can throw ~5 tiles. If we build the launcher 5 tiles from the enemy's border infrastructure, the thrown builder lands IN their network. The "too short" assessment may have been about reaching the enemy core (which is deep in their territory). But reaching their peripheral conveyors is much more feasible.

The key mechanic: a builder can c.fire(pos) to attack enemy buildings on its own tile for 2 damage, or c.destroy(pos) to instantly remove allied buildings. But against enemy buildings, each attack costs 2 Ti + cooldown. However, the disruption value isn't the 2 damage -- it's that the builder standing on a building means turret attacks hit the builder, not the building. A decoy builder absorbs 30 HP of enemy turret fire while teammates dismantle infrastructure.

Actually, the real play: builder self-destruct does no damage. So the attack is just the 2-damage fire action. Against 20 HP conveyors, that's 10 rounds per kill. Against pure economy bots with NO turrets (One More Time), even 1 builder in their network is devastating -- no turrets to stop it.

### Risk
- Launcher is 20 Ti + 10% scale -- significant investment
- If enemy has gunners, the thrown builder dies immediately
- Builder attack is very slow (2 damage/round, 10 rounds per conveyor)
- Our own economy suffers from diverting builders to attack
- Already marked as dead end... but for a different reason (core destruction range, not infrastructure disruption)

### How to test
1. Build "timing_attack" variant: at round 500, convert 2 builders to attackers targeting enemy conveyors (not core)
2. Test vs ladder_eco (no defense) -- should be devastating
3. Test vs ladder_rush (has defense) -- expect it to fail
4. Key question: is the Ti spent on attack recovered by denying enemy economy?

### Expected Elo impact
+5 to +15 Elo. Narrow use case (only works against defenseless economy bots). But against those specific bots, it could flip losses to wins. Low priority compared to fixing our own economy.

## Unconventional Ideas (Speculative, Not Fully Developed)

### A. "Conveyor Trap" -- Build conveyors facing TOWARD enemy core near the map border
Resources on conveyors move toward the output direction. If we build conveyors that point toward the enemy, and the enemy builds their conveyors adjacent, resources from OUR harvesters could flow INTO enemy buildings -- but the reverse is also true. Could we position conveyors to steal enemy resources? The game warns: "Resources CAN be sent to enemy buildings via misplaced conveyors." Could we exploit this intentionally?

**Verdict:** Too risky. If it goes wrong, we feed the enemy. But worth investigating the mechanic.

### B. "Builder Bot Shield" -- Park builders on critical buildings
"If a builder bot stands on a building, turret attacks on that tile hit ONLY the builder bot." This means a builder bot is a 30 HP shield for any building. Could we park spare builders on key harvesters or conveyors near the front line to absorb turret fire?

**Verdict:** Cheap defense (builder costs 30 Ti, has 30 HP). But it permanently ties up a builder. Better to build a barrier (3 Ti, 30 HP) for 10x the efficiency.

### C. "Marker Communication Protocol" -- Use markers for builder coordination
Currently markers only encode "ore claimed" (value=1). We have a full u32 (4 billion values). We could encode: builder count in sector (top 4 bits), nearest ore distance (next 8 bits), enemy sighting (1 bit), chain health (1 bit). This would give every builder a shared map awareness without globals.

**Verdict:** High potential, high complexity. The marker encoding scheme needs careful design. But this is the ONLY way to achieve coordination in this game. Worth exploring after Wave 1.

### D. "Foundry-Free Refined Ax" -- Skip foundry, just mine more Ti
Foundry has +100% scale cost. What if instead of building a foundry for refined Ax (needed for armoured conveyors and breach turrets), we simply mine more Ti and never touch Ax? Our current bot already ignores Ax, and this seems correct given the foundry tax. But if we ever need breach turrets or armoured conveyors, we need to find a way to make the foundry math work.

**Verdict:** Current approach (ignore Ax) is likely correct for our Elo range. Revisit only if we reach 1600+ and need breach turrets to compete.

## Key Insight from This Analysis

**Our #1 problem is NOT finding ore. It's delivering ore to core.**

We build plenty of harvesters. We explore well (after v33 sector diversity fix). But our conveyor chains are leaky -- resources get stuck in dead-end conveyors that don't connect back to core. The d.opposite() approach creates chains that follow the builder's wandering path, and diagonal movement creates gaps that silently disconnect the chain.

Every strategy that improves chain connectivity (Chain Census, Hub and Spoke) has the highest expected impact. Every strategy that adds more spending (Spend Down, Mirror Mining) amplifies whatever chain connectivity we already have. And every offensive strategy (Ammo Network, Timing Attack) only matters once our economy is competitive.

**Fix the pipes, then turn up the pressure.**
